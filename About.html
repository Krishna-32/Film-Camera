A film camera, also known as an analog camera, is a traditional photographic device that captures images on photographic film.

Unlike digital cameras, film cameras use rolls of light-sensitive film to record pictures.

When you take a photo with a film camera, light enters through the camera's lens and exposes the film,

creating a latent image. To view the photographs, the film must be developed in a darkroom or by a professional lab,

a process that involves chemical development, fixing, and printing.

Film cameras are renowned for their unique and timeless aesthetic, offering a hands-on and nostalgic approach to photography,

with each shot requiring careful consideration and precision.

While digital photography has become more prevalent, film cameras continue to be cherished by enthusiasts and artists for their

ability to capture the world in an authentic and tangible way, preserving the art of analog photography.
